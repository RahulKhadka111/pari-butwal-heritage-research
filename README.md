# 🏛️ Pari Butwal Heritage Research

This project started with my grandma's stories and a pull toward the old houses in my city that I couldn't quite explain. I'm not a history student — this was an independent research project I took on because I wanted to do something that mattered for my community. 

Over months of walking through Pari Butwal, sometimes 10km in a single day, I interviewed elderly residents and pieced together a history that's never really been written down: Jitgadhi Fort's role in the Anglo-Nepalese War, the temples and masjids that have stood side by side here for generations, and the ancient houses that are quietly disappearing. The fort and the houses gave me the topic. The people gave me the reason. Their stories are what I actually walked away with.

---

## 📋 Research Scope & Key Focus Areas
* **Jitgadhi Fort:** Its role in the Anglo-Nepalese War, the local memory of the Gorkhali Sena's stand there, and the state of preservation today.
* **Ancient Architecture:** The 350+ year-old traditional houses of old Batauli — their structural design and what's being lost as they disappear.
* **Interfaith Coexistence:** The Hindu and Muslim communities of the area, and how that coexistence has been sustained across generations.
* **The Developmental Gap:** The contrast — historical and present-day — between the ancient settlement of Batauli and the modern hub of Khasauli.

---

## 🔍 Methodology
This research was built primarily on oral history — interviews with elderly residents and community members who carry firsthand or generational knowledge of the area. Where possible, these accounts were cross-checked against public records and local news coverage (*myRepublica*, *Envedo*) to verify historical timelines. The final paper was written and typeset entirely in LaTeX.
